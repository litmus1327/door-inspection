Based on the video, here is a description of how the zoom functionality works:

*   **Zoom to Cursor:** The zoom is directly tied to the position of the cursor. When the user initiates a zoom, the application identifies the specific point on the drawing that is currently under the cursor.
*   **Centering and Positioning:** As the user zooms in, the drawing is not just magnified; it is also repositioned. The application automatically pans the drawing so that the point originally under the cursor is moved towards the center of the screen.
*   **Following the Cursor:** By the time the zoom action is complete, the area that was targeted by the cursor has become the central focal point of the view. For example, in the video, the user places the cursor over the "ELEV EL6A" area and zooms in. The view adjusts so that this specific elevator section is brought to the center of the screen, making it the primary area of focus.
*   **Zooming Out:** When zooming out, the process reverses, gradually revealing more of the drawing until the entire floor plan is visible again, returning to the initial centered state.
